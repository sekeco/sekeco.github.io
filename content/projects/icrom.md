We developed ICROM to simplify academic event operations by centralizing registration, participant management, paper submission, reviewer workflows, and admin monitoring in one platform. System replaces scattered spreadsheets, email threads, and document folders with structured operational dashboard.

## Key Features

- **Event Setup and Registration Management**
    - Built tools for creating multiple events, registration windows, and thematic structures.
    - Standardized event setup so organizers can launch programs faster.
    - Reduced repeated manual configuration across seminars and conferences.

- **Participant Management and Document Handling**
    - Linked participants to events with support for registration evidence and supporting files.
    - Added bulk evidence download to speed up administrative verification.
    - Reduced manual handling of payment proofs and eligibility documents.

- **Paper Submission and Author Collaboration**
    - Supported submissions with title, abstract, publication target, and uploaded paper or presentation files.
    - Enabled multi-author collaboration for academic and professional papers.
    - Organized submission flow around event and theme context.

- **Reviewer Assignment and Structured Scoring**
    - Implemented reviewer assignment workflow with scoped reviewer access.
    - Added structured evaluation criteria such as theme fit, novelty, data depth, and legibility.
    - Improved consistency and transparency across review process.

- **Operational Control and Visibility**
    - Added role-based access for admins, reviewers, and related stakeholders.
    - Included notifications, activity logging, export support, and dashboard statistics.
    - Gave operational teams clearer visibility into submission and review progress.

## Impact and Success

ICROM turned fragmented academic event coordination into structured digital workflow. Platform improved organizer efficiency, made reviewer workflows more consistent, and gave institutions stronger operational control, auditability, and scalability for conferences and research programs.
