1 Rumah Journal is a digital publishing platform initiative built for PT Cendikia Nusantara Insani to present and scale its academic journal ecosystem. At its core, this project addresses a clear business challenge: many research institutions and journal publishers have valuable scientific content, but their public-facing websites often fail to communicate trust, quality, and ease of use. Authors get confused about submission pathways, readers struggle to discover relevant journals, and institutions miss opportunities to strengthen reputation in the academic market.

This product solves that gap by turning journal information into a clear, conversion-oriented experience. Instead of simply listing pages, it creates a guided narrative for different stakeholders: prospective authors who want to publish, researchers looking for indexed references, and institutional partners evaluating credibility. The result is a website platform that feels both professional and approachable, with a strong emphasis on editorial transparency, journal positioning, and user confidence.

## Product Purpose

From a business perspective, this platform is designed to help an academic publisher grow trust and participation in its journal ecosystem. It supports three strategic goals:

- Increase manuscript submissions by making journal options and publication flows easier to understand.
- Strengthen institutional brand perception through a polished, consistent, and credible digital identity.
- Improve discoverability of publications and editorial standards so the organization appears ready for broader national and international collaboration.

In short, this is not just a website redesign. It is a communication and growth asset for the publisher’s long-term positioning.

## Key Features

- **Unified Multi-Journal Showcase**  
  The platform introduces three journal verticals (Humaniora, Sains & Teknologi, and Ekonomi) within one cohesive experience. Each journal has its own dedicated page with distinct positioning, helping users immediately understand where their research fits.

- **Author Journey and Submission Guidance**  
  The site presents a step-by-step publication process, from registration to review and final publication. This reduces friction for first-time contributors and supports stronger conversion from visitor interest to submission intent.

- **Editorial Credibility Layer**  
  Dedicated sections highlight editorial board structure, review approach, indexing claims, publication ethics, and issue cadence. These elements function as trust signals, essential for attracting higher-quality submissions and institutional confidence.

- **Content-Rich Journal Presentation**  
  Journal pages are structured like product pages: scope topics, sample current issues, DOI-style references, publication schedule, and APC context. This makes the platform useful for both audience education and practical decision-making.

- **Mobile-Ready, Brand-Forward Experience**  
  Navigation, layouts, and call-to-action components are designed for both desktop and mobile users. Visual consistency across pages gives the publisher a premium presence suitable for modern academic communication.

## Value Proposition

This product is useful because it combines branding, communication, and conversion in one scalable package.

For institutional leadership, it turns journal operations into a stronger public brand that looks established and future-ready. For authors, it removes uncertainty by clearly explaining what to submit, where to submit, and what to expect from the review process. For readers and partners, it provides a clean gateway to understand journal scope, quality signals, and publication outputs.

The most important value is strategic: it helps transform a journal organization from “information available online” into “a credible research platform people trust and want to engage with.” In competitive publishing environments, that trust can directly influence submission volume, collaboration opportunities, and long-term academic reputation.

## Current Status

Based on the available files, this project is in a **high-fidelity MVP / pre-production stage**.

It already includes:

- Complete multi-page website architecture.
- Structured templates for core pages and all three journal categories.
- Reusable layout components (navigation, footer, journal layout).
- Build and deployment workflow for generating static production output.
- Ready-to-publish visual and content direction.

At the same time, several elements indicate this is not yet fully mature production software:

- Contact and search flows are still presentation-first (some are placeholders/simulated behavior).
- Several links and interactions are designed as future integration points.
- No evidence of analytics instrumentation, CMS/editor integration, or automated testing pipeline.

This is a very strong foundation: polished enough for stakeholder demonstrations, portfolio showcase, and controlled launch, while still open for the next growth phase.

## Next Development Opportunity

The highest-impact next move is to evolve this from a showcase platform into a **fully integrated journal operations front-end**.

That means connecting the current experience with real editorial systems (such as OJS workflows), activating real form handling and article feeds, and introducing measurement layers (analytics dashboards, conversion tracking, and SEO optimization). With those additions, the platform can shift from “excellent digital presence” to “measurable acquisition and publishing engine.”

From a portfolio perspective, this project already demonstrates strong product thinking: clear audience targeting, compelling institutional storytelling, and a scalable interface system. The next iteration can position it not only as a beautiful academic web platform, but as a true business growth channel for scholarly publishing.
